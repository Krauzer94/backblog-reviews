This RE entry is a standout in the series, blending classic survival horror with a deeper narrative and improved 3D graphics over its predecessors. The MC is Claire Redfield and later Chris, exploring eerie environments filled with puzzles, limited resources, and terrifying creatures. ## Ambitious Narrative Depth
One of the strongest aspects of this entry is its ambitious storytelling, which pushes the series forward in meaningful ways. The game delves deeper into the Redfield siblings‚Äô backstory and expands the overarching Umbrella narrative, introducing memorable antagonists and more personal stakes than earlier entries. ## Refined Survival Horror
As for the gameplay, this game retains the methodical pacing that defines classic survival horror, rewarding careful exploration and smart resource management. There is no real big changes from the previous PlayStation 1 entries on this regard. The puzzles are more involved and often require attention to detail, encouraging you to truly engage with the environments rather than rush through them. Although some design choices can feel punishing, they also reinforce the constant tension and sense of vulnerability. Overall, the game represents a high point of classic RE design, bridging the gap between the old-school formula and the series‚Äô future evolution.
